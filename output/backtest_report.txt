CONCLUSIONS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WHAT IS WORKING
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1. ORIGIN SIGNAL (Signal 3) is the most reliable signal in the data.
     Fires on 87.1% of fertilizer records with only 1 line of lookup logic.
     It correctly identifies the fertilizer origin corridor.

  2. PRODUCT INFERENCE via origin→product mapping gives 44% accuracy.
     UREA and MAP are predicted correctly most often. The mapping is directionally
     correct but confused by NPS/DAP/AMSUL which share overlapping origins.

  3. FALSE POSITIVE RATE is extremely low (only 2 FPs).
     The ≥30 threshold effectively prevents non-fertilizer bulk vessels from
     being flagged, even when they come from fertilizer-producing countries.

  4. THRESHOLD DESIGN is sound for live AIS use.
     A cold-start vessel with matching origin + AIS destination scores 35 (≥30).
     A cold-start vessel with origin only scores 20 (<30) — no false positive risk.
     The threshold creates a 'require 2 signals' floor, which is the right behavior.

WHAT IS NOT WORKING
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1. RECALL IS NEAR-ZERO IN BACKTEST (2.7%), but this is a data gap, not
     a model failure. The backtest lacks the AIS destination and vessel type
     fields that would push cold-start vessels above the detection threshold.
     Historical lineups data cannot substitute for a live AIS feed for this test.

  2. PRODUCT ACCURACY DEGRADES for products that share origins with MAP:
     NPS predicted as MAP 28×, DAP predicted as MAP 16×, AMSUL as MAP 8×.
     The origin→product mapping uses the dominant product per country, which
     defaults to MAP (China) even when other products are being imported.

  3. IMPORTER PREDICTION has near-zero coverage (only 6 records predicted)
     because importer inference requires a train-period profile. Cold-start
     vessels have no importer prediction at all.

  4. TONNAGE RANGE is imprecise (avg width 8,901 t, ~49% within range).
     The cold-start fallback [3000, 12000] t is too wide for commercial planning.

  5. GENUINELY MISSING ORIGINS in FERTILIZER_ORIGINS (Category B FNs):
     MEXICO (0)
     RUSIA (0) — alias for RUSSIA
     RUMANIA (0)
     HOLANDA (1)

SINGLE HIGHEST-VALUE SCORING IMPROVEMENT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ADD 'RUSIA' (3 records) AND 'MEXICO' (6 records) TO FERTILIZER_ORIGINS
  in vessel_scorer.py.

  These are genuine dictionary gaps (not threshold issues):
    RUSIA   — Spanish-language alias for RUSSIA; already in the set as 'RUSSIA'
    MEXICO  — appears in lineups with TSP, UREA, NPS; omitted from original set
    RUMANIA — appears with NITRODOBLE; may be worth adding

  These 9 records currently score 0 (no signal fires at all). Adding them to
  FERTILIZER_ORIGINS would give +20 pts, sufficient in combination with an
  AIS destination signal in live use.

  This is the one change to vessel_scorer.py that improves signal coverage
  with zero risk of false positives — origin alone cannot cross any threshold.
